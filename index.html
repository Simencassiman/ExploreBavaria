About

Don't know what to do around Munich? Don't worry! There's plenty of things to keep you busy. On this site you can find a few things to get you started. There are many cities around that are worth visiting. Also the nearby Alps provide beautiful hikes if that's more your thing.


Some useful things

For starters, moving through Munich and its immediate surroundings is very easy with the semester ticket you immediately have as a student. This allows you to move freely using any means of public transport between 6pm and 6am, and any time during the weekend. If you feel like this is not enough, you can always purchase the IsarCard. Very handy if you're willing to pay the almost 200€. Both these tickets are valid withing all six MVV zones of Munich.

If your're planning to move further away from Munich with some friends, but still within Bavaria, the Bayern Ticket is a very handy option. It allows for up to five passengers, the first one costs 25€, every additional passenger (2-5) costs an extra 7€. This ticket is also valid for any means of transport, so if you need to take a bus after your train ride, no problem!

If you're looking for more hikes around the area (or even for other places) Komoot is a very handy place to start. it offers a lot of options with good information on the routes. Here you can also look up biking routes and it offers an app so you can have the detailed paths on your phone.

There's definitely no shortage of activities!
